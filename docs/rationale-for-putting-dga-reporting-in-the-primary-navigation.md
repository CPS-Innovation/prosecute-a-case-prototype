Hey mate, wanted to share my thinking on DGA reporting being in the primary nav before we chat later:
 
It's a top-level area of the service - not a sub-feature of cases, not a detail view, just its own thing that some users need to do.
 
Currently the nav has Overview, Cases, Directions, Tasks, Prosecutors, Paralegal officers, and Activity - although a number of those are just in my prototype/future vision, not in live. Either way, DGA reporting fits naturally alongside those.
 
We did research with the link to DGA reporting within the list of cases, and some participants missed it. To be fair, that’s not surprising - it's hard to spot and does not naturally reflect the user’s mental model having clicked cases - naturally we’d expect users to be thinking about cases. Related: some participants naturally went to the case list to process cases that failed DGA - that’s fine but it’s only a small part of the journey - recording outcomes, not managing the DGA process as a whole which involves multiple steps before they get to a filtered view of the case list.
 
I also don't think the answer is making the link more prominent within the case list (turning the link into a button, for example). It's a link to a top-level section, not an action the user is taking from within the case list.
 
I also don't think it should be (just a link) on the overview page. The overview is there to help users understand what they should work on next, not to put top-level navigation items.
 
Note: the DGA reporting section would only be shown to users who need it, so it won't add noise for everyone.
